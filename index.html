Originality

Some of the best hacks have been ones that are completely original. Often, novel uses of sponsor APIs fall under this category. At a Hackathon, we had used the Twilio and Google Maps API to find free toilets and we ended up winning because of that. With our Javascript/jQuery skills, it isn’t be too difficult to integrate API(s) and produce something fun and useful.

Perspective

Our pitch put our solution into perspective for the judges. Many hackathons have some sort of mission, be it democratising artificial intelligence, enabling better use of public data or some corporate goal, we are able to define how our solution achieves that goal and articulate it within 2 minutes (length of most presentations).

Perseverance

Often, the code won’t work or the documentation will be unmaintained or difficult to understand. It is important that we don’t give up. At such times, we often get up, go for a walk, talk to other hackers, get help from the mentors. But, we never leave a hack halfway, we get some feature of it to work and then reflect on what we could have done differently. This makes for an incredible learning experience.

Flexibility

Some times, the project we may have taken up would just not be feasible in the 24/36h time frame and this is when you and our team is flexible enough to move things around or come up with new ideas entirely.
